Overview

FIFA’s World Cup App was designed as a companion app for the tournament which took place in 2014 in Brazil. By the mid-point of the games, “more than 2.5 billion screen views had been consumed, with fans spending an average of 54 minutes each interacting with live, editorial and social content. By the final whistle, the app had become the most downloaded sports event app of all time with over 28 million downloads, #1 in over 200 countries” and the 2014 tournament being acknowledged as the ‘first truly mobile and social World Cup’.”

Background & Role

In 2013 I was invited to work on the official app for FIFA alongside Grapple Mobile’s team of fantastic designers, developers, and product managers. A member of a cross-functional team, I was responsible for leading the high level concept, conducting a deep-dive discovery process, as well as delivery of the main experience of the app for mobile & tablet on iOS and Android.

Project Goals

Together with FIFA we identified three main goals for the app:

  1. Users should be engaged with the app despite stream of games being unavailable in-app. The engagement should happen before, during and after the games.
  2. The app should be valuable and enjoyable for both hardcore football fans and people tuning in just for the event.
  3. App should encourage user’s perception of FIFA a socially responsible and inclusive brand, highlighting organisation’s social and charitable work around the world

Discovery & Process

The team was locked up in a room for a week trying to break the requirements down into a meaningful user experience and high-level concept. The sessions were organised in way which encouraged collaboration and creative input from all members of the team including managers and developers. I find that doing this allows every department to feel involved and excited about the project, fostering a greater team spirit-which is what often drives the concept through to delivery.

Guiding Concepts

After 5 days of creative sessions, we came up with three concepts we wanted to guide this project. We wanted to make sure we have a point of reference later on in delivery that we could look back to to asses priority of features. These were:

I. Global Stadium

II. Game On

III. Responsible Brand

Global Stadium

Driving engagement with the app without live steaming was the main focus of our sessions. We asked ourselves what else make the tournament the global event it is and how can we make the app useful for people watching the games at home. Our user research suggested that fans love the conversation happening around the games almost as much as the game itself—with vast majority posting passionately on Twitter, Facebook and Instagram. We expected this behaviour to intensify as the Cup enveloped, coming from both people watching games at home and those experiencing matches at stadiums.

This sparked the idea of a Global Stadium-a space in the app where the social content from around the world can be experienced by all users, with smart algorithms highlighting the most relevant content to each fan. The app would prioritise content by user’s preference, favourite teams and country of origin, putting focus on the social content arriving from the actual stadiums and exclusive behind the stage official footage to allow people watching at home feel as close as possible to actually being there.

Game On

Another pilar for the app was the idea that we should attempt to recreate the dynamic feel and rich interactions of football games UI such as FIFA by EA Sports and TV sports channels to make the consumption of large amount of information feel fun and an experience in itself for the fans. We looked particularly towards Eurosport and various other American sports channels featuring dramatic animations and airy ambience. We paid attention to transitions, contextual UI, and used fake blur effect (mind you this was before iOS7) to create a sense of depth and context. Diagonal lines running through the design were a mindful continuation of FIFA’s logo and a reminder of the dynamic nature of sports. We even considered modelling navigation after design elements familiar from the pitch and built a prototype of a hidden game that users could activate with magic gesture (neither ended in the final product).

We also carefully considered Information Architecture of the countless stats and match updates which helped us design a clean news reader and football database where fans and casual users alike can easily navigate, find and understand the information they encounter. We also ensured it was easy to your favourite teams and subscribe to match reminders.

Responsible Brand

Finally we asked ourselves how can we engage people who come to the app for entertainment into an educational piece about FIFA’s work in charity and help craft the perception of the brand as socially responsible and inclusive of everyone. We looked for inspiration to children’s educational iPad apps (one of we which another team has been working on at the same time) and the WWF fantastic environmental iPad app, a winner of Apple’s annual design award in 2013. We found that the best way to educate a distracted mind is through a compelling interactive, rich storytelling, which we did by creating a series of simple tasks that unlock consecutive pieces of information.

Another way of making everyone feel one with the global brand was through a design that worked across platforms and in all the supported languages without a compromise to the experience. This meant making sure that no user felt second-best because they have an older device or poor internet connection.

Launch & Reception

At the time the app launched and World Cup officially kicked off, I was celebrating my 30th birthday in Iceland. Watching the Live Draw with the FIFA companion app on my lap, I forgot all the sleepless nights and effort that when into this project, and simply enjoyed the fact the app was now available for all to download. The app reach number 1 in 200 countries, generated over 2 billion screen views and 28 million downloads generating favourable reception from the fans and design community.

Afterword—3 years on

It’s now 3 years since we were commissioned to work on this project. While I still feel this is one of best projects I’ve worked on to date, I can’t help but scrutinise some of the decisions we made. In particular, if given a chance, I would refine the use of iconography and redesign main navigation to help drive traffic to all the sections of the app. I would also give more attention to the notifications strategy which I feel has become a platform in itself for modern brands. Looking forward to the next version of the app for the 2018 World Cup in Russia.

Special thanks to everyone who’s worked on this project. It was an amazing privilege to see your tireless work ethic and craftsmanship. Till next time!
